Central Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985:
- Section 14: Jurisdiction, powers, and authority of the Central Administrative Tribunal (CAT) over service matters.
- Section 15: Jurisdiction, powers, and authority of State Administrative Tribunals.
- Section 22: CAT not bound by Code of Civil Procedure, guided by natural justice.
- Section 28: Exclusion of jurisdiction of all courts except Supreme Court.
- Section 29: Transfer of pending cases to the Tribunal.
Key case: S.P. Sampath Kumar v. Union of India (1987): Upheld the Act's constitutional validity.
