Article 14: Equality before law and equal protection of laws. No person shall be denied equality before the law or the equal protection of the laws within the territory of India.
Key principle: Prohibits arbitrary discrimination by the State. Any classification must be reasonable and have a rational nexus with the object sought to be achieved.
Landmark cases: State of West Bengal v. Anwar Ali Sarkar (1952), E.P. Royappa v. State of Tamil Nadu (1974).
